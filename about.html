Machine Learning in Diabetes Prediction

The Dataset

Our prediction model is trained on the Pima Indians Diabetes Database, a well-known dataset in the machine learning community. It contains data from 768 women of Pima Indian heritage, all at least 21 years old.

  • 768 patient records
  • 8 input features
  • Binary classification
  • Extensively studied

The Algorithm

We use a Logistic Regression model, which is excellent for binary classification problems like diabetes prediction. The model learns patterns from historical patient data to predict future outcomes.

  • High accuracy rate
  • Fast predictions
  • Easy to interpret
  • Proven effectiveness

Understanding the Input Features

Age

The age of the patient in years. Diabetes risk increases significantly after age 45.

Low Risk: < 35 Medium: 35-44 High Risk: 45+
Glucose Level

Plasma glucose concentration - the primary indicator of diabetes.

Normal: < 100 Prediabetic: 100-125 Diabetic: 126+
Blood Pressure

Diastolic blood pressure (mm Hg). High BP is linked to diabetes complications.

Normal: < 80 Elevated: 80-89 High: 90+
BMI

Body Mass Index - a measure of body fat based on height and weight.

Normal: 18.5-24.9 Overweight: 25-29.9 Obese: 30+
Insulin

2-Hour serum insulin (μIU/mL) - measures the pancreas's insulin production.

Normal: < 16 Elevated: 16-166 High: 166+
Skin Thickness

Triceps skin fold thickness (mm) - an indicator of body fat percentage.

Normal: < 20 Medium: 20-35 High: 35+
